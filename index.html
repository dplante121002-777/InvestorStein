Full Phone Pitch Script (Glen)

Glen can literally read this during the call if he wants. It’s written in natural spoken language.

Section 1

Opening Statement – 30 seconds

Hook
Thanks for taking the time today, I’ll keep this tight and direct. Men’s and women’s vitality care is broken, underserved, and exploding in demand. We’re building the first dual-gender telehealth ecosystem that solves the real underlying issues – not just the surface symptoms. For men, the decline usually starts quietly in their thirties – testosterone drops, energy and motivation fall, recovery slows, mood shifts, confidence slips. For women, peri-menopause and menopause create hormonal chaos, emotional disconnect, low desire, sleep issues, and intimacy challenges that affect the entire household. This is not a vanity problem. It’s an identity problem. People want to feel like themselves again.
Section 2

The Market Gap

Why now
The market is exploding, but there is no true category leader. Hims and Hers are largely surface-level – digital marketing machines focused on simple prescriptions. Legacy hormone clinics are outdated, inconvenient, expensive, and mostly focused on men. Women’s vitality – especially peri-menopause and menopause – is still massively underserved. Nobody has built a trusted, compliant, dual-gender vitality brand that serves both sides of the household under one medical umbrella. That’s the space we’re taking with MensRx and WomensRx.
Section 3

Founder Positioning – Vision + Compliance

Glen’s lane
Let me give you thirty seconds on my background. I bring over thirty years of healthcare experience, and my lane sits at the intersection of vision and compliance. On the compliance side, I’ve built and managed multi-state provider networks, telehealth systems, and pharmacy and compounding partnerships. My specialty is building the operational and regulatory frameworks that keep medical operations safe, legal, and scalable. On the visionary side, I’ve always paid attention to where healthcare is moving before it arrives there. Back in 2017 – long before the telehealth boom – I secured the MensRx and WomensRx trademarks because I saw the shift coming in male and female vitality medicine. That early positioning gives us defensible IP, a strong brand foundation, and a head start most companies simply don’t have. So my role is simple: I run the medical vision and the compliance backbone, making sure everything we build is future-focused, innovative, and aligned with regulatory standards as we scale.
Section 4

The Solution – Two Connected Platforms

Product

Keep it simple here – men / women / household.

Our solution is two connected platforms. MensRx is the male vitality side – testosterone optimization, peptides, libido and performance, energy, recovery, and mood support. WomensRx is focused on peri-menopause and menopause – hormone balance, desire and intimacy pathways, emotional and hormonal support, with personalized compounding where appropriate. We treat the root causes, not just the symptoms. The goal for patients is simple: “I feel like myself again.”
Section 5

Science & Safety

De-risk
Our protocols use FDA-approved molecules, evidence-based compounding, and provider-guided pathways. Every patient interaction is structured around safety, personalization, and compliance. This is not a pill-of-the-month subscription company. This is a clinical vitality ecosystem with real medical oversight.
Section 6

Competitive Advantages

Why us
  • IP Ownership: MensRx and WomensRx trademarks secured in 2017 – early, defensible brand position.
  • Compliance Infrastructure: We build for scale, not shortcuts. Compliance is baked into the model from day one.
  • Dual-Gender Household Capture: We serve both men and women under one trust banner, which naturally increases retention and LTV.
Section 7

Go-to-Market

Growth
Our acquisition model is direct and scalable: influencer partnerships, user-generated content, organic education, paid search, and SEO-driven demand. Vitality medicine is a high-intent category – people are actively searching for these solutions every day. We control the acquisition engine internally so we’re not dependent on a single outside agency.
Section 8

Financial Shape & Exit Path

Upside
Financially, this is a path to fifty to one hundred million in revenue within three to five years with strong gross margins in the seventy to eighty-five percent range, driven by recurring subscription revenue on both sides of the household. Strategically, we’re a direct acquisition target for players like Hims, Hers, Ro, or private-equity healthcare rollups once we prove scale. If an investor passes today, they’re likely to see MensRx and WomensRx everywhere over the next few years.

Quick Investor Q&A Cheat Sheet

Glen can glance here when an investor interrupts with a tough question.

Most Likely Questions
Q: Doesn’t Hims/Hers already do this?
A: They do a small slice. They’re brilliant at digital marketing and simple prescriptions, but they live mostly at the surface level. We’re focused on deeper clinical vitality – full hormone work-ups, long-term protocols, and a true dual-gender household model.
Q: Why hasn’t anyone clearly won this category yet?
A: It requires two things most teams don’t have together – real clinical credibility and the ability to scale compliantly across states. Most players are either strong marketers or strong clinicians, but not both. Our advantage is the combination of vision, compliance experience, and early IP.
Q: Why you, personally?
A: My background is thirty-plus years building and managing provider networks, telehealth systems, and pharmacy and compounding partnerships. My lane is building things that are both innovative and compliant, so investors aren’t betting on a marketing gimmick – they’re betting on a durable medical platform.
Q: What’s the real moat here?
A: 2017 trademarks for MensRx and WomensRx, a compliance-first infrastructure, and our ability to capture both sides of the household under one brand. Once a couple trusts us for their vitality care, the switching cost is extremely high.
Q: What’s the biggest risk?
A: The biggest risk is scaling faster than the systems, which is true in every telehealth business. That’s exactly why we treat compliance as the backbone rather than a footnote – we’re designing the model to handle scale without cutting corners.

Call Flow Notes for Glen

  • Step 1: Open with the pain and the identity line – “People want to feel like themselves again.”
  • Step 2: Briefly explain the gap – surface-level vs clinical-level, men only vs dual-gender.
  • Step 3: Position yourself – vision + compliance, 2017 trademarks.
  • Step 4: Walk through MensRx and WomensRx in simple language – don’t get too technical.
  • Step 5: Hit the three pillars – IP, compliance infrastructure, dual-gender household capture.
  • Step 6: Explain acquisition model and financial shape at a high level.
  • Step 7: End with the exit path and let them ask questions. Use the Q&A section above.
