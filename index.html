Full Phone Pitch Script (Glen)

Section 1

Opening Statement – 30 seconds

Hook
Thanks for taking the time today, I’ll keep this tight and direct. Men’s and women’s vitality medicine is going through a major shift right now. The problem is that most of what people see online only addresses surface symptoms. What’s missing is a clinical, compliant approach that treats the real causes behind hormone decline, energy loss, intimacy challenges, and the emotional and physical disconnect that so many people quietly deal with. That’s the gap we’re filling with MensRx and WomensRx. For men, the decline usually begins silently in their thirties – testosterone drops, energy and motivation fall, recovery slows, mood shifts, confidence slips. For women, peri-menopause and menopause create hormonal imbalances, disrupted sleep, emotional strain, and often a major drop in desire and connection. It affects relationships, confidence, and quality of life. This is not vanity care. It’s identity care. People want to feel like themselves again.
Section 2

The Market Gap

Why now
The market is clearly there – Hims and Hers and others have proven demand in this space. Where we see the gap is at the clinical vitality layer. Most of the big direct-to-consumer brands live at the surface level: quick visits, simple prescriptions, subscription refills. They are optimized for speed and convenience. What doesn’t really exist yet is a trusted, clinical vitality ecosystem for both men and women – with hormone optimization, compounding options, provider-guided protocols, and a long-term relationship with the household. That’s the lane we’re in with MensRx and WomensRx.
Section 3

Founder Positioning – Vision + Compliance

Glen’s lane
Let me give you thirty seconds on my background. I bring over thirty years of healthcare experience, and my lane sits at the intersection of vision and compliance. On the compliance side, I’ve built and managed multi-state provider networks, telehealth systems, and pharmacy and compounding partnerships. My specialty is building the operational and regulatory frameworks that keep medical operations safe, legal, and scalable. On the visionary side, I’ve always paid attention to where healthcare is moving before it arrives there. Back in 2017 – long before the telehealth boom we’re seeing now – I secured the MensRx and WomensRx trademarks because I saw where the vitality and hormone space was heading. That early positioning gives us defensible IP, a strong brand foundation, and a head start most companies simply don’t have. So my role is simple: I run the medical vision and the compliance backbone, making sure everything we build is future-focused, innovative, and aligned with regulatory standards as we scale.
Section 4

The Solution – Two Connected Platforms

Product

Keep it simple here – men / women / household.

Our solution is two connected platforms. MensRx is the male vitality side – testosterone optimization, peptides, libido and performance, energy, recovery, and mood support. WomensRx is focused on peri-menopause and menopause – hormone balance, desire and intimacy pathways, emotional and hormonal support, with personalized compounding where appropriate. We treat the root causes, not just the symptoms. The goal for patients is simple: “I feel like myself again.”
Section 5

Science & Safety

De-risk
Our protocols use FDA-approved molecules, evidence-based compounding, and provider-guided pathways. Every patient interaction is structured around safety, personalization, and compliance. This is a clinical vitality ecosystem, not a pill-of-the-month subscription company.
Section 6

Competitive Advantages

Why us
  • IP Ownership: MensRx and WomensRx trademarks secured in 2017 – early, defensible brand position.
  • Compliance Infrastructure: We build for scale, not shortcuts. Compliance is our backbone, not a footnote.
  • Dual-Gender Household Capture: We serve both men and women under one trust banner, which naturally increases retention and lifetime value.
Section 7

Go-to-Market

Growth
Our acquisition model is direct and scalable: influencer partnerships, user-generated content, organic education, paid search, and SEO-driven demand. Vitality medicine is a high-intent category – people are actively searching for these solutions every day. We control the acquisition engine internally so we’re not dependent on a single outside agency.
Section 8

Financial Shape & Exit Path

Upside
Financially, this is a path to fifty to one hundred million in revenue within three to five years, with strong gross margins driven by recurring subscription revenue on both sides of the household. Strategically, we are a natural acquisition target for players like Hims, Hers, Ro, or private-equity healthcare rollups once we prove scale. The demand is proven – our advantage is the clinical depth and dual-gender household model.

Quick Investor Q&A Cheat Sheet

Glen can glance here when an investor interrupts with a tough question.

Most Likely Questions
Q: Doesn’t Hims/Hers already do this?
A: They do a slice of it, mostly at the surface level – quick visits, simple prescriptions, subscription refills. Our model is built around clinical vitality: hormone optimization, peptides, compounding, and long-term protocols for both men and women. Clinics and deeper clinical programs require a different operational backbone – provider oversight, lab monitoring, liability coverage, and heavier state regulation. Hims and Hers are optimized for speed and simplicity. We’re optimized for clinical depth and outcomes.
Q: Why hasn’t anyone clearly won this category yet?
A: Because it requires two skill sets that rarely exist in the same team – strong clinical credibility and the ability to scale compliantly. Most companies lean heavily to one side or the other. Our advantage is the combination of early IP, clinical experience, and a compliance-first design.
Q: Why you, personally?
A: I’ve spent over thirty years in healthcare building provider networks, telehealth systems, and pharmacy and compounding partnerships. My lane is vision plus compliance – building something innovative in a way that’s medically safe and scalable. Securing MensRx and WomensRx back in 2017 is an example of seeing where the category was going early on.
Q: What’s the real moat here?
A: Early IP with MensRx and WomensRx, a compliance-first infrastructure, and dual-gender household capture. Once a household trusts us for both partners’ vitality care, the switching cost is high and the long-term value is strong.
Q: What’s the biggest risk?
A: The biggest risk is always scaling faster than the systems. That’s why we’re designing compliance and infrastructure as the backbone from day one, rather than bolting them on later.
