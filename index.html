Full Phone Pitch Script (Glen)

Section 1

Opening Statement – 30 seconds

Hook
Thanks for taking the time today, I’ll walk you through this clearly and directly. Men’s and women’s vitality medicine is going through a major shift right now. The problem is that most of what people see online only addresses surface symptoms. What’s missing is a clinical, compliant approach that treats the real causes behind hormone decline, energy loss, intimacy challenges, and the emotional and physical disconnect that so many people quietly deal with. That’s the gap we’re filling with MensRx and WomensRx. For men, the decline usually begins silently in their thirties – testosterone drops, motivation and recovery slow, mood and confidence change. For women, peri-menopause and menopause create hormonal swings, disrupted sleep, emotional strain, and often a major drop in desire and connection. This isn’t vanity care. It’s identity care. People want to feel like themselves again.
Section 2

The Market Gap

Why now
The market is clearly there – Hims, Hers, and others have already proven demand. But they operate mostly at the surface level: quick visits, simple prescriptions, and subscription refills. What doesn’t exist yet in a meaningful way is a clinical vitality ecosystem that serves both men and women, with real hormone optimization, compounding options, provider-guided protocols, and a long-term relationship with the household. That’s the lane we’re in with MensRx and WomensRx.
Section 3

Founder Positioning – Vision + Compliance

Glen’s lane
Let me give you thirty seconds on my background. I’ve been in healthcare for more than thirty years, building and managing multi-state provider networks, telehealth systems, and pharmacy and compounding partnerships. My lane is combining vision with compliance – building things that are innovative, but also medically safe and scalable. Back in 2017, long before the telehealth boom we’re seeing today, I secured the MensRx and WomensRx trademarks because I saw where the vitality and hormone space was heading. That early IP gives us a defensible brand position that most competitors simply don’t have. So my role is simple: I run the medical vision and the compliance backbone, making sure everything we build is future-focused, clinically sound, and aligned with regulatory standards as we scale.
Section 4

The Solution – Two Connected Platforms

Product

Keep it simple here – men / women / household.

Our solution is two connected platforms under one clinical vitality brand. MensRx focuses on male vitality – testosterone optimization, peptides, libido and performance, energy, recovery, and emotional stability. WomensRx focuses on peri-menopause and menopause – hormone balance, emotional regulation, desire and intimacy pathways, and personalized compounding where appropriate. These are medically guided protocols. The simple goal on the patient side is: “I feel like myself again.”
Section 5

Science & Safety

De-risk
Our protocols use FDA-approved molecules, evidence-based compounding, and licensed providers. Everything is structured around safety, personalization, and compliance. This is a clinical vitality ecosystem, not a pill-of-the-month subscription company.
Section 6

Competitive Advantages

Why us
  • IP Ownership: MensRx and WomensRx trademarks secured in 2017 – early, defensible brand position.
  • Compliance Infrastructure: We build for scale, not shortcuts. Compliance is the backbone, not a footnote.
  • Dual-Gender Household Capture: We serve both men and women under one trusted umbrella, which naturally increases retention and lifetime value.
Section 7

Go-to-Market

Growth
Our acquisition model is direct and scalable: influencer partnerships, user-generated content, organic education, paid search, and SEO-driven demand. Vitality medicine is a high-intent category – people are actively searching for these answers every day. We control the acquisition engine internally so we’re not dependent on a single outside agency.
Section 8

Financial Shape & Exit Path

Upside
Financially, we see a path to fifty to one hundred million dollars in annual revenue within three to five years, with strong gross margins driven by recurring subscription revenue on both sides of the household. From an exit standpoint, we are a natural acquisition target for players like Hims, Hers, Ro, or private-equity healthcare rollups once scale is demonstrated. The space is proven – the clinical depth and household model are what differentiate us.

Hims/Hers Positioning – How Glen Should Answer

Key Comparison

Glen should never say “we’re first” or deny Hims/Hers. He should position them as proof the market is big – and explain calmly why your model is deeper and more clinical.

Short Answer Glen Can Memorize

“Hims and Hers are great marketers. They’ve proven that the demand is there. Where we’re different is that we’re building what they’re not set up to do – a clinically guided vitality ecosystem for both men and women, with hormone optimization, peptides, compounding, and longer-term protocols. Their strength is convenience and simple prescriptions. Our strength is clinical depth, personalization, and outcomes.”

Why They Don’t Have Clinics – Glen’s Answer

“Clinics require a very different operational backbone – in-person provider oversight, lab monitoring, liability frameworks, and state-by-state clinical compliance. Hims and Hers are structured for speed and simplicity. Their model is optimized for quick visits and scalable telehealth, not clinic infrastructure. If they moved into clinics at scale, they’d be in a different business model entirely. That’s the space we’re designed for – a more regulated, medically intensive model that we can build safely around compliance and systems from day one.”

Quick Investor Q&A Cheat Sheet

Glen can glance here when an investor interrupts with a tough question.

Most Likely Questions
Q: Isn’t Hims/Hers already doing men and women?
A: They are, but at the surface level. Their model is built around convenience and simple prescriptions. We’re focused on clinical vitality – deeper protocols, hormone optimization, compounding, and long-term care for both men and women under one brand.
Q: Why don’t Hims/Hers have physical clinics?
A: Clinics require a different operational backbone – provider presence, monitoring, liability coverage, and heavier state regulation. Their business is optimized for speed and simplicity. Clinic infrastructure would slow them down and change their risk profile. Our model is designed to handle that level of compliance and oversight from day one.
Q: Why you, personally?
A: I’ve spent over thirty years building multi-state provider networks, telehealth systems, and pharmacy and compounding relationships. My lane is vision plus compliance – building something innovative in a way that’s medically safe and scalable. MensRx and WomensRx were trademarked back in 2017 because I saw early where this space was heading.
Q: What’s the real moat here?
A: Early IP, a compliance-first infrastructure, and dual-gender household capture. Once a household trusts us for both partners’ vitality care, the switching cost is very high and the lifetime value is strong.
Q: What’s the biggest risk?
A: The biggest risk is always scaling faster than the systems. That’s exactly why we’re building compliance and infrastructure as the backbone – not as an afterthought.
